Brain Comparison Analysis

Profile

Healthy Brain

    • Alzheimer’s Brain

    • Key Differences

    Healthy Brain
    • Normal hippocampal volume
    • Strong neural synaptic connections
    • Normal amyloid-beta levels
    • Preserved cognitive & memory function
    • Stable reasoning, learning & attention
    • No neurofibrillary tau tangles
    • Balanced neurotransmitter levels
    • Stable cortical thickness & brain mass
    • Efficient neural communication
    Alzheimer’s Brain
    • Significant hippocampal shrinkage
    • Weakened or lost synaptic connections
    • High amyloid plaque accumulation
    • Severe memory & cognitive decline
    • Tau protein tangles disrupt neurons
    • Thinned cortical tissue (atrophy)
    • Reduced neurotransmitter activity
    • Slowed information processing
    • Lower brain metabolism (glucose use)

    Analytical Insights

    Memory Decline Rate

    Alzheimer’s patients show 40–60% faster decline in hippocampal memory formation ability.

    Neural Connectivity Loss

    Up to 30% of synaptic connections deteriorate, slowing reasoning, learning and responsiveness.

    Amyloid Plaques

    Beta-amyloid protein buildup blocks neuron communication and triggers inflammatory damage.

    Tau Tangles

    Tau proteins twist into toxic tangles, collapsing internal neuron transport systems.

    Cortical Atrophy

    Alzheimer’s brains lose 10–20% cortical mass in decision-making and memory regions.

    Neurotransmitter Imbalance

    Reduced acetylcholine and dopamine disrupt attention, focus and overall cognition.
